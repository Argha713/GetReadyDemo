SSC GD Selection Process 2025, CBT, PET, PST, Medical Exam

  • Admin
  • Oct 9, 2024

The SSC GD Selection Process 2025 consists of four stages, the first stage is the SSC GD Written Exam, followed by the Physical Efficiency Test (PET)/Physical Standards Test (PST) tests. Selected candidates will further appear for the SSC GD Constable Medical Examination and Document Verification.

SSC GD Selection Process

The Staff Selection Commission issued the SSC GD Notification 2025 on 5th September 2024 on the official SSC website at ssc.gov.in. Candidates who want to apply online for General Duty Constable posts should know the SSC GD Selection Process 2025. This blog provides a clear overview of the SSC GD Constable Selection Process 2025, including important exam dates and other key details.

SSC GD Selection Process 2025

The Selection Process of the SSC GD Recruitment 2025 consists of three main stages i.e. Computer Based Test (CBT), Physical Efficiency Test, and Medical Test. Candidates need to qualify for all the stages of the examination to get recruited for the released Vacancies.

Stage Description
Written Examination Multiple-choice questions on General Intelligence & Reasoning, General Knowledge & Awareness, Elementary Mathematics, and English/Hindi.
Result Declaration Results will be posted on the SSC website; qualified candidates will proceed to the PET and PST exams.
PET/PST Includes tests such as running and measurement of physical standards (height, chest, etc.).
Medical Examination Ensures candidates meet the required health standards for the position.
Document Verification Involves verifying educational certificates, identity proof, and other necessary documents.
Final Result Based on performance in PET/PST and the medical exam; the final merit list is published accordingly.

SSC GD Constable Selection Process

Knowing about the exam pattern gives the candidates a clearer idea of how the examination will be structured. The SSC GD Constable Exam Pattern is given below in detail.

SSC GD Selection Process 2025 – Computer-Based Exam

The SSC GD Constable Computer-Based Examination is an objective-type test aimed at evaluating candidates’ knowledge in various subjects.

  • Total Number of Questions: 80
  • Marks per Question: 2
  • Total Marks: 160
  • Exam Duration: 60 minutes (1 hour)
  • Type of Questions: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
  • Language Options: English or Hindi
  • Marks for Correct Answers: 2 marks
  • Negative Marking: 0.25 marks deducted for each incorrect answer
  • Level of Questions: Matriculation Level
Subject No. of Questions
General Intelligence & Reasoning 20*2
General Knowledge & General Awareness 20*2
Elementary Mathematics 20*2
English/ Hindi 20*2
Total 80*2

SSC GD Selection Process 2025 – PET And PST

Candidates will be shortlisted for the SSC GD Physical Test comprising of the Physical Efficiency Test (PET) and Physical Standard Test (PST) based on their performance in the Computer-Based Examination (CBE).

  • Shortlisting: Candidates are selected for the Physical Efficiency Test (PET) and Physical Standards Test (PST) based on their performance in the Computer-Based Examination (CBE).
  • Test Locations: PET and PST will be conducted at locations decided by the Central Armed Police Forces (CAPFs).
  • Document Verification: Qualifications and documents are checked during the Detailed Medical Examination (DME).
  • Eligibility Verification: Candidates must confirm their eligibility as per the notification before attending the CBE, PST, or PET.
  • PET (Race) & Identification: Candidates who meet the height standards will take the PET (race) and undergo biometric identification.
  • PST Scrutiny: After passing the PET (race), CAPFs will review eligibility for any relaxation during the PST.

SSC GD Selection Process 2025 – Medical Examination

The SSC GD Constable Physical Standard Tests are followed by the SSC GD Physical Efficiency Test and the Medical Standard Test. Candidates can download the details of the SSC GD Medical Standards through the direct link provided below.

Health Requirement Description
Mental Health Candidates must be intelligent and maintain good mental health.
Hearing Hearing should be clear, with no significant ear, nose, or throat issues.
Vision Vision in each eye must meet standards, and eyes should be clear and healthy.
Speech Speech should be smooth and free from stammering.
Glandular Swelling No visible swelling in any glands on the body.
Chest The chest should be well-formed, with no abnormalities, and should expand at least 5 cm. Heart and lungs must be healthy.
Limbs and Joints Limbs, hands, and feet must be fully developed, with free movement in all joints.
Old Fractures No signs of old or improperly healed fractures should be present.
Joint Movement Joints must move freely without any issues.
Feet and Toes Feet and toes should be properly formed.
Congenital Defects No visible birth defects or malformations should be present.
General Health No history of past diseases or health problems that may affect fitness.
Teeth Candidates must have at least 14 healthy teeth for proper chewing.
Genitourinary Health No visible diseases in the genitourinary system.
Scrotal Health No swelling should be present in the scrotum or inguinal area.
Testes Testes should be located in the scrotum and of normal size (for male candidates).
Re-enrolment History Past medical history must be checked for any previous injuries or health issues for re-enrolment.

SSC GD Selection Process 2025 – Document Verification

Among the candidates who have qualified for the PET/ PST, some candidates will be shortlisted for the Detailed Medical Examination (DME). The SSC will verify the applicable documents at the time of DME. A few of them are mentioned below.

  1. Matriculation Certificate: Confirms age, name, and educational qualifications.
  2. Domicile Certificate: Proof of residency issued by the relevant authority.
  3. Valid NCC Certificate: Required if applicable.
  4. Defence Personnel Certificate: As specified in Annexure-IV of the SSC GD Notification 2025.
  5. Undertaking for Ex-Servicemen: As detailed in Annexure-V of the SSC GD Notification 2025.
  6. Caste Certificate: Needed if applying for reservation or age relaxation, following formats in Annexure-VI, VII, and VIII of the SSC GD Notification 2025.
  7. Height/Chest Relaxation Certificate: For candidates requesting relaxation, as per Annexure-IX of the SSC GD Notification 2025.
  8. Certificate for Riot Victims: Issued by District Collector/District Magistrate, as mentioned in category 4/5/6 under Para 5.1 of the SSC GD Notification 2025.
  9. Nativity/Identity Certificate: For West Pakistani Refugees, in the format provided in Annexure-XII of the SSC GD Notification 2025.
