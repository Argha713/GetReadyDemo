SSC GD Notification 2025 – Educational Qualifications

To apply for the SSC GD Constable Recruitment 2025, candidates must meet certain educational requirements as of 1st January 2025. The details are provided below:

  1. Educational Qualification: Candidates must pass the 10th Class or Matriculation Exam from a recognized Board/University by January 1, 2025.
  2. Ineligibility: Those who do not have the required qualification by January 1, 2025, cannot apply.
  3. Open and Distance Learning (ODL): Degrees, diplomas, or certificates from ODL programs are accepted if the institution is approved by the Distance Education Bureau (DEB) of the UGC for the relevant period.
  4. Document Verification for ODL: ODL candidates must provide proof of approval from the Distance Education Bureau, UGC, during the Document Verification process.
  5. Document Verification: All candidates must present original documents, such as Mark Sheets and Certificates, to verify their qualifications before January 1, 2025.
  6. Result Declaration Requirement: Only candidates with results declared by January 1, 2025, are eligible. Processing of results by the Board or University does not count.

How To Apply Online For The SSC GD Constable Notification 2025?

The process of filling out the online application for the SSC GD 2024-25 examination consists of two main parts:

  1. One-time Registration
  2. Filling out the Online Application for the Examination

SSC GD One-Time Registration (OTR) Process:

  1. Visit SSC Website: Go to the official SSC website or use the direct link provided.
  2. Login or Register: Click on “Apply.” If you are a new user, select “Register Now.”
  3. Start Registration: Begin the registration by clicking “Register Now.”
  4. Enter Details: Fill in your Aadhaar number, name, father’s name, mother’s name, and date of birth (twice for verification). Errors will be shown in red.
  5. Verify Mobile and Email: Enter the OTP sent to your mobile and email to verify.
  6. Save Registration: Click “Save Next.” Your Registration Number will appear on the screen and be sent to you. Complete the process within 14 days.
  7. Complete Registration: Click “Continue” to go to the login page.
  8. Finish Later: You can complete the process later by logging in with your Registration Number and password.
  9. Create a Password: Set a new password during your first login.
  10. Enter Additional Details: Fill in details like category, nationality, and address. Non-Indian citizens should provide contact information.
  11. Provide Identification and Disability Info: Enter identification marks and disability details if applicable (Disability Certificate Number is needed).
  12. Submit Declaration: Read and agree to the declaration, then click “Declare.”
  13. Final Step: Log in with your new Registration Number and password to complete the SSC GD Application Form 2025.

SSC GD Constable Application Form 2025:

  1. Visit SSC Website: Go to the official SSC website and click “Apply Online.”
  2. Start the Application: Click on “Apply Online” to begin.
  3. Register (If Not Registered): If you are not already registered, click “Register Now.”
  4. Complete Registration: Fill in all the required details in the registration form.
  5. Log In: Log in to your account with your registration number and password.
  6. Fill Application Form: Click on “Apply” and enter the necessary details in the form.
  7. Upload Documents: Upload scanned copies of your photograph and signature (ensure a live photo is taken using a laptop or phone camera).
  8. Review and Submit: Carefully check the form for errors, then click “Final Submit.”
  9. Pay the Application Fee: Pay Rs. 100 for General category candidates. Female, SC, ST, and PWD candidates do not need to pay. Payment can be made online or offline.

SSC GD Constable Application Correction 2025

The Staff Selection Commission (SSC) provides a window for candidates to correct any errors in their Online Application Form after submission. This SSC GD Application correction window will remain active between the 5th to 7th of November 2024 allowing candidates to make necessary changes before final submission. Below are the key details regarding the process and guidelines for making application corrections.

  1. Correction Window: Active from November 5 to November 7, 2024, for candidates to modify and re-submit their online application forms.
  2. Number of Corrections Allowed: Candidates can correct and re-submit their application forms up to two times during the correction window.
  3. Eligibility for Correction: Only candidates who submitted their completed application form with the fee on time can make corrections.
  4. Correction Charges: ₹200 for the first correction and ₹500 for the second correction, applicable to all candidates, regardless of gender or category.
  5. Payment Mode: Correction charges must be paid online using BHIM UPI, Net Banking, Visa, Mastercard, Maestro, or RuPay debit cards.
  6. Non-Refundable Charges: Correction charges are non-refundable and cannot be used for any other exam or selection.
  7. Valid Application: The latest modified application will be considered valid, and all previously submitted applications will be canceled.
  8. Incomplete Application: If correction charges are not paid, the application will be marked as ‘Incomplete,’ and the previous application will remain valid.
  9. Final Check: Candidates must ensure all details are correct before submitting the corrected form, as no further changes will be allowed after the correction window.
  10. No Post-Correction Requests: No requests for corrections will be accepted after the correction window via post, fax, email, or other forms of communication.

SSC GD Final Selection Criteria 2025

The SSC GD 2025 final selection for recruitment involves several steps and considerations. The details are provided below in the table:

1. Final Selection

  • The final selection will be based on the normalized marks obtained in the Computer-Based Examination (CBE), which includes NCC bonus marks.
  • Candidates will be selected based on their state, category, and specific areas such as Border Guarding or regions affected by Militancy or Naxal activities.
  • For positions in SSF (Sashastra Seema Bal) and NCB (Narcotics Control Bureau), selection will occur on an All-India basis.

2. Area-Specific Consideration

  • Candidates from Naxal or militancy-affected areas will be prioritized for positions in those specific regions.
  • Candidates from border areas will receive priority for roles in border locations.

3. General Conditions

  • Candidates must meet all eligibility criteria at every stage of the selection process.
  • Successfully clearing the Computer-Based Test (CBT) and the Physical Examination does not guarantee an appointment unless all conditions are satisfied.
  • If selected candidates do not receive any communication within one year, they are advised to reach out to the relevant CAPF (Central Armed Police Forces) or organization for clarification.

4. Representation

  • If any candidate encounters issues regarding the results, they must contact the Regional Office within two months of the announcement or two weeks before the next selection stage.

SSC GD Constable Marks Calculation Process

The SSC GD Constable Marks Calculation Process is crucial for candidates who want to understand how their scores are determined in the SSC GD examination. Knowing how to calculate marks helps candidates assess their performance and set goals for improvement. The calculation process is straightforward, based on correct and incorrect answers. Below are the details of the marking scheme.

Aspect Details
Total Questions 80
Total Marks 160
Marks for Correct Answer +1 Mark per question
Marks for Incorrect Answer -0.25 Marks per wrong answer
Marks for Unanswered Questions 0 Marks
Final Score Calculation Final Score = (Total correct answers × 1) – (Total incorrect answers × 0.25)

SSC GD Constable 2025 – NCC Bonus Marks

The SSC GD Constable 2025 NCC Bonus Marks is an important aspect for candidates applying for the SSC GD examination. Candidates holding an NCC certificate can receive additional marks, which can enhance their chances of qualifying for the exam.

Particular Marking Scheme
NCC ‘C’ Certificate CBT Final Score + 5 Marks (5% of Maximum Marks of the CBT Exam)
NCC ‘B’ Certificate: CBT Final Score + 3 Marks (3% of Maximum Marks of the CBT Exam)
NCC ‘A’ Certificate: CBT Final Score + 2 Marks (2% of Maximum Marks of the CBT Exam)
  1. Eligibility: Candidates must opt for incentive/bonus marks for an NCC certificate in the Online Application Form.
  2. No Change Allowed: If a candidate does not indicate NCC certificate status in the form, they cannot change it later, even if they have the certificate.
  3. Verification Required: The benefit for NCC certificate holders will be confirmed only if they provide the original certificate during Document Verification.
  4. Provisional Award: Bonus marks will be provisionally awarded based on the candidate’s claim in the Online Application Form until the NCC Certificate is verified.
  5. Crucial Date: The NCC Certificate must be issued on or before 01-01-2025 to qualify for the bonus marks.
  6. Ex-Servicemen Exemption: This benefit is not available for Ex-Servicemen.

How to Calculate Your SSC GD CBT Exam Final Score?

  1. Count the Correct Answers: Add up all the questions you answered correctly.
  2. Count the Incorrect Answers: Add up all the questions you answered incorrectly.
  3. Use the Formula:
    • Multiply the total correct answers by 1 (since each correct answer gives 1 mark).
    • Multiply the total incorrect answers by 0.25 (since each incorrect answer deducts 0.25 marks).
  4. Calculate Your Final Score: Subtract the marks deducted for incorrect answers from the total marks gained for correct answers.
  5. Specially For NCC Certificate Holder: NCC ‘C’ Certificate add 5 Marks to your CBT Final Score. NCC ‘B’ Certificate add 3 Marks to your CBT Final Score. NCC ‘A’ Certificate add 2 Marks to your CBT Final Score.

What After SSC GD Constable CBT Exam 2025?

After the SSC GD Constable CBT Exam, there are several key steps in the selection process, ensuring candidates are evaluated thoroughly both on their written performance and physical capabilities.

  1. Tentative Answer Key Release – SSC will release a tentative answer key after the exam, allowing candidates to review their answers and estimate their scores.
  2. Objection Window – Candidates will have a specific time to raise objections if they find errors in the tentative answer key, providing valid proof for the challenge.
  3. Final Result and Cut-off Scores – After reviewing objections, SSC will announce the SSC GD CBT Result 2025 with the list of selected candidates and the official cut-off score, the minimum qualifying score.
  4. Physical Efficiency Test (PET) – Candidates who will clear the SSC GD CBT Exam will appear for the PET, which tests their physical stamina through tasks like running, long jumps, and other exercises as per SSC guidelines.
  5. Physical Standard Test (PST) – Candidates must also pass the PST, which ensures they meet physical measurements such as height, chest (for males), and weight, as required by SSC.

What Is the SSC GD Physical Test(PET/PST)?

The Physical Standard Test (PST), Physical Efficiency Test (PET), Detailed Medical Examination (DME), Review Medical Examination (RME), and Document Verification (DV) will be organized and conducted by the Central Armed Police Forces (CAPFs).

The SSC GD Constable PET/PST exam evaluates the physical fitness of candidates based on standards set by the SSC and conducted by the CRPF.

  • Physical Standard Test (PST): Measures height, weight, and chest size.
  • Physical Efficiency Test (PET): Requires candidates to complete a race within a set time limit.
  • Passing Criteria: Candidates must clear both the PET and PST to move to the next stage.
  • Next Steps: After passing PET and PST, candidates proceed to the Detailed Medical Exam (DME) and Document Verification (DV).

SSC GD 2025 Medical Examination

The SSC GD Constable Physical Standard Tests are followed by the SSC GD Physical Efficiency Test and the Medical Standard Test. The details of the basic SSC GD Medical Examination are provided in the table below. Candidates can also download the SSC GD Medical Standards using the direct link provided.

  1. Mental Health: Candidate should be intelligent and have good mental health.
  2. Hearing: Hearing must be clear, with no noticeable ear, nose, or throat problems.
  3. Vision: Vision in each eye should meet the required standards, and eyes must be clear and normal.
  4. Speech: Speech should be smooth and free from stammering.
  5. Glandular Swelling: There should be no visible swelling in any glands on the body.
  6. Chest: Chest should be well-formed with no abnormalities and should expand by at least 5 cm. The heart and lungs must be healthy.
  7. Limbs and Joints: Limbs, hands, and feet should be fully developed, with free movement in all joints.
  8. Old Fractures: No signs of old or improperly healed fractures should be present.
  9. Joint Movement: Joints must move freely without any issues.
  10. Feet and Toes: Feet and toes should be properly formed.
  11. Congenital Defects: No visible birth defects or malformations should be present.
  12. General Health: No history of past diseases or health problems that may affect fitness.
  13. Teeth: Candidate should have at least 14 healthy teeth for proper chewing.
  14. Genitourinary Health: There should be no visible disease in the genitourinary system.
  15. Scrotal Health: No swelling should be present in the scrotum or inguinal area.
  16. Testes: Testes should be in the scrotum and of normal size (for male candidates).
  17. Re-enrolment History: For re-enrolment, the candidate’s past medical history must be thoroughly checked for any previous injuries or health issues.
