Banking Exam Eligibility

Banking exams are a popular choice among aspirants looking to build a career in the banking sector. The eligibility criteria for different banking exams may vary based on the nature of the exam and the organization conducting it. Here are the eligibility criteria for some of the popular banking exams in India:

  • SBI PO: To be eligible for the SBI PO exam, candidates must have a graduation degree in any discipline from a recognized university. The age limit for the exam is between 21 to 30 years. However, there are age relaxations for candidates belonging to certain categories.
  • RBI Group B: The eligibility criteria for the RBI Group B exam require candidates to have a minimum of 60% marks in their graduation. The age limit for the exam is between 21 to 30 years. However, there are age relaxations for candidates belonging to certain categories.
  • SBI Clerk: To be eligible for the SBI Clerk exam, candidates must have a graduation degree in any discipline from a recognized university. The age limit for the exam is between 20 to 28 years. However, there are age relaxations for candidates belonging to certain categories.
  • SBI SO: The eligibility criteria for the SBI SO exam require candidates to have a graduation degree in the relevant field. The age limit for the exam varies based on the post applied for. However, there are age relaxations for candidates belonging to certain categories.
  • IBPS Clerk: The eligibility criteria for the IBPS Clerk exam require candidates to have a graduation degree in any discipline from a recognized university. The age limit for the exam is between 20 to 28 years. However, there are age relaxations for candidates belonging to certain categories.
  • IBPS RRB: To be eligible for the IBPS RRB exam, candidates must have a graduation degree in any discipline from a recognized university. The age limit for the exam is between 18 to 30 years. However, there are age relaxations for candidates belonging to certain categories.
